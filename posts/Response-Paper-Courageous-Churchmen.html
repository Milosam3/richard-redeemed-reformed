Response Paper on Courageous Churchmen

I never want to think of myself as one who should have a role of leadership and authority in the context of the church body. This book has solidified that thinking and encouraged me to keep asking, learning and wanting to grow in my spiritual maturity. I recognise at the same time that I am called to lead my wife and family and I want to excel at that. The first two chapters were very impactful to me considering the message of living in such a way that is worth following and what is my core belief driving the way in which I live. I believe James 1:22 summarises it perfectly: “But prove yourselves doers of the word, and not merely hearers who delude themselves.”. If I am not soaking myself in the word of God and then being a doer of God’s word, I am not only deceiving myself but those around me as well. This verse has almost haunted me for the last 12 months, in a good way, because I do not want to be all talk and no conviction, no action. I want to live a life of integrity and trustworthiness, one that is honouring God. I have played rugby since I was 6 years old. It's a combat sport, there is no backing down or showing weakness. Meekness was not a word I have been very familiar with. Being meek sounds weak, but it is a Godly character trait which encompasses humility. Humble leadership is the good example you see throughout the scriptures. As Jerry points out, Moses’s life was a story about God, not Moses. Jesus, the Son of God, came to serve not be served. This point has convicted me to be more conscious about the needs of those around me than that of my own. If I want to strive to be any sort of leader I must put others first and consider how I am able to serve those around me for the cause of the gospel. As Jerry points out, “we are utterly insignificant”, this has allowed me to see clearly that it's not about me and my heart's desires but rather what God wants for me and those around me. I need to get out of the way and prioritise the souls of those I encounter or have influence over. Being intolerant is a very selfish trait and one God opposes. We are called to be selfless. The middle of chapter 6 unpacks this point and it hits home as I often find myself being impatient with people around me when they are not “getting it right” as I expect them to. I am being intolerant when an event or individual changes the order of my day from how I had planned it. Closely following this poor attitude as Jerry writes next in his book is anger, self-righteousness and judgemental attitudes. Unpacking these thoughts has been convicting to me. It has helped me be more conscious of these negative traits and led me to prayer, pleading with God for grace to overcome them and die to self more. When people are involved, there is inevitably conflict. Just because we are all part of the body of Christ does not mean we will all always get along. Sin is ever present, ready to cause division and strife. The beautiful thing about the body of Christ though is that we are/should be equipped to handle conflict. This section has made me think more about human differences and unique personalities. Previously I would have had an unrealistic expectation that we all should behave and interact in a certain way because we all love Jesus so why wouldn't we. I have come to better understand that, despite our common love, there are still elements or as Jerry says, "common diversity” relating to the way God has put us together that can/will result in conflict, despite our best efforts. Further to understanding this, I have been able to take a practical approach by praying for God to reveal my flaws and ways I may cause conflict because of my “common diversity”. These points have allowed me to embrace trials, knowing that God is sovereign and that despite the tribulation, I am in His perfect will. I am now eager to seek the lessons in my trials to further grow in my spiritual maturity. Lastly, with handling conflict I was encouraged to read about separating personal preferences from biblical principles. This point I consider fundamental to effective conflict resolution.
